Texas Chili

1x

"True Texas Chili" recipe from Lobel's Meat Bible.

Ingredients

  • 70 g (1 pack) dried whole New Mexico (California), guajillo, or pasilla chiles (6 to 8 chiles)
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper
  • Kosher salt
  • 5 tbsp lard, vegetable oil, or rendered beef suet
  • 1 kg beef chuck
  • 1 finely chopped onion
  • 3 large garlic cloves
  • 2 cups beef stock
  • 2 1/4 cups water, plus more as needed
  • 2 tbsp masa harina
  • 1 tbsp packed dark brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tbsp distilled white vinegar
  • Sour cream
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Toast chiles in cast iron until fragrant, 2-3 minutes per side. Soak chiles in hot water until soft, 15-45 minutes, turning once.
  2. Chili paste: Drain the chiles, split and remove stems and seeds. Mix together chiles, cumin, black pepper, 1 tbsp kosher salt (1/2 tbsp table salt), 1/4 cup water. Blend into a smooth paste, adding more water as needed.
  3. Beef: Lightly brown the beef in the lard in 2 batches.
  4. Saute onion and garlic for 3-4 minutes.
  5. Simmer: Add in the stock, remaining 2 cups water, and masa harina. Add the chile paste. Add the beef and any juices and bring to a simmer.
  6. Slow cook around 4 hours.
  7. Stir in the brown sugar and vinegar, add salt to taste.
  8. Rest: Turn off heat and let chili stand for 30 minutes to thicken.
  9. Reheat and serve with sour cream and lime.
