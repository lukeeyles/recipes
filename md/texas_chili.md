## Texas Chili

### Ingredients

* _70_ g (1 pack) dried whole New Mexico (California), guajillo, or pasilla chiles (_6_ to _8_ chiles)
* _1 1/2_ tsp ground cumin
* _1/2_ tsp ground black pepper
* Kosher salt
* _5_ tbsp lard, vegetable oil, or rendered beef suet
* _1_ kg beef chuck
* _1_ finely chopped onion
* _3_ large garlic cloves
* _2_ cups beef stock
* _2 1/4_ cups water, plus more as needed
* _2_ tbsp masa harina
* _1_ tbsp packed dark brown sugar
* _1 1/2_ tbsp distilled white vinegar
* Sour cream
* Lime wedges 

### Directions

1. Toast chiles in cast iron until fragrant, 2-3 minutes per side. Soak chiles in hot water until soft, 15-45 minutes, turning once.
2. Drain the chiles, split and remove stems and seeds. Mix together chiles, cumin, black pepper, _1_ tbsp salt (_1/2_ tbsp table salt), _1/4_ cup water. Blend into a smooth paste, adding more water as needed.
3. Lightly brown the beef in the lard in 2 batches.
4. Cook onion and garlic for 3-4 minutes. Add the stock, remaining _2_ cups water, and masa harina. Add the chile paste Add the beef and any juices and bring to a simmer. Slow cook around 4 hours.
5. Stir in the brown sugar and vinegar, add salt to taste. Turn off heat and let chili stand for 30 minutes to thicken.
6. Reheat and serve with sour cream and lime.
