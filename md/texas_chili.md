title: Texas Chili
date: 25/05/26

"True Texas Chili" recipe from Lobel's Meat Bible.

### Ingredients

* _70_ g (_1_ pack) dried whole New Mexico (California), guajillo, or pasilla chiles (_6_ to _8_ chiles)
* _1 1/2_ tsp ground cumin
* _1/2_ tsp ground black pepper
* Kosher salt
* _5_ tbsp lard, vegetable oil, or rendered beef suet
* _1_ kg beef chuck
* _1_ finely chopped onion
* _3_ large garlic cloves
* _2_ cups beef stock
* _2 1/4_ cups water, plus more as needed
* _2_ tbsp masa harina
* _1_ tbsp packed dark brown sugar
* _1 1/2_ tbsp distilled white vinegar
* Sour cream
* Lime wedges 

### Instructions

1. **Toast chiles** in cast iron until fragrant, 2-3 minutes per side. Soak chiles in hot water until soft, 15-45 minutes, turning once.
2. **Chili paste**: Drain the chiles, split and remove stems and seeds. Mix together chiles, cumin, black pepper, _1_ tbsp kosher salt (_1/2_ tbsp table salt), _1/4_ cup water. Blend into a smooth paste, adding more water as needed.
3. **Beef**: Lightly brown the beef in the lard in 2 batches.
4. **Saute** onion and garlic for 3-4 minutes. 
5. **Simmer**: Add in the stock, remaining _2_ cups water, and masa harina. Add the chile paste. Add the beef and any juices and bring to a simmer. 
6. **Slow cook** around 4 hours.
7. Stir in the **brown sugar and vinegar**, add salt to taste. 
8. **Rest**: Turn off heat and let chili stand for 30 minutes to thicken.
6. Reheat and serve with sour cream and lime.
